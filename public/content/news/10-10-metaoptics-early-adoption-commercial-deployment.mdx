    <div className="mdx-text">
        <strong>1.  What is MetaOptics’ business about, and what are its key revenue segments?</strong>
        <ul class="list-disc pl-5">
            <li class="pl-5">
            MetaOptics is a vertically integrated semiconductor optics company specialising in glass-based metalens technologies. Unlike traditional lenses that use curved glass, our metalenses are flat, with semiconductor photolithography processes used to directly etch millions of pillars onto the glass, allowing for thin lenses.
            </li>    
            <li class="pl-5">
            We operate in four main business segments: a) Metalens production equipment and testing; b) Metalens production; c) Metalens IoT products; and d) Imaging and IoT algorithms to optimise metalens performance.
            </li>    
            <li class="pl-5">
            Our capabilities span capital equipment, 4inch prototyping and 12inch foundry platforms, metalens products, and MetaOptics AI. Revenue is generated from design, prototyping, and wafer level manufacturing, serving industries such as AI, consumer electronics, sensing, and medical devices. 
            </li>    
            <li class="pl-5">
            We are planning to build the world’s first 12inch glass foundry. We want to be a leading edge metalens solutions provider to world class customers.
            </li>    
        </ul>
    </div>

    <div className="mdx-text">
        <strong>2. What is the company’s customer acquisition and retention strategy?</strong>
        <ul class="list-disc pl-5">
            <li class="pl-5">
            MetaOptics works closely with customers from design to integration, embedding its metalens solutions into next generation products early in the development cycle.
            </li>    
            <li class="pl-5">
            This co-development approach strengthens retention, as customers typically scale into repeat manufacturing orders once products move to commercialisation. 
            </li>    
            <li class="pl-5">
            For instance, we recently shipped our Automatic Metalens Tester to a partner in Taiwan complementing our previous deployment of MetaOptics’ Direct Laser Writer last September. Building a robust metalens manufacturing ecosystem in Taiwan with our DLW and Automatic Metalens Tester allows us to serve customers more effectively.
            </li>    
        </ul>
    </div>

    <div className="mdx-text">
        <strong>3. What direction is the metalens market moving towards and how does MetaOptics plan to navigate it?</strong>
        <ul class="list-disc pl-5">
            <li class="pl-5">
            The market is shifting toward wafer-level, miniaturised optics integrated into next generation devices. 
            </li>    
            <li class="pl-5">
            Key use cases for metalenses include mobile phone cameras, biometric sensors as well as co-packaged optics for chips that are utilised for AI. 
            </li>    
            <li class="pl-5">
            MetaOptics partners with customers to design, evaluate, and integrate metalenses into future products, positioning itself as a long-term technology partner rather than a component supplier. We recently completed a projector metalens design for a South Korean consumer electronics customer.
            </li>  
            <li class="pl-5">
            We are designing metalens for world class customers for their next generation consumer products and air particle sensors.  
            </li>   
        </ul>
    </div>

    <div className="mdx-text">

            <li class="pl-5">
            Through these collaboration and partnerships, we will be developing solutions for these world class customers.

        <strong>6. How is MetaOptics integrating ESG considerations into its strategy?</strong>
        <ul class="list-disc pl-5">
            <li class="pl-5">
            MetaOptics’ glass based metalenses are more durable and heat resistant than conventional plastic lenses, supporting longer product lifecycles.
            </li>    
            <li class="pl-5">
            Its wafer level manufacturing also enables more efficient material usage and production, aligning sustainability with performance and cost.
            </li>   
        </ul>

Metalens can be implemented in consumer electronics, automotive, industrial and emerging applications in the field of co-packaged optics and fibre optics communications.
